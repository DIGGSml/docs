Element diggs:LinearGroutingActivityType / diggs:groutingType
Namespace http://diggsml.org/schemas/3
Annotations
 
Specifies the grouting treatment solution for this
activity. Allowed values are: Compaction - injecting a very stiff
homogeneous grout mix under relatively high-pressures and at low
injection rates to subsurface locations in pre-designed patterns in
order to displace and compact soils. Compensation - also know as
fracture grouting, is the injection of a cement slurry grout,
hydrofracturing soil, creating lenses. The lenses compact
surrounding soil and then lift the overlying soil and structures.
Jet - consists of breaking up the soil with a high-pressure jet in a
predrilled borehole and mixing the loosened soil with a cement grout
alone or mixed with air and/or water. Permeation - Introduction of
cement or chemical-based grout into soil pores without an essential
change in the original soil volume and structure, changing the
support capability of granular soils without disturbing them Rock -
the injection of specially formulated cement-based mixes into the
ground to improve its strength or reduce permeability.

Element diggs:LinearGroutingActivityType / diggs:cavity_depth
Namespace http://diggsml.org/schemas/3
Annotations
 
For grouting in a borehole, typically done for jet or
deep soil mixing, the length of the top of the hole that is
pre-drilled out completely to form a cavity to contain the
additional volume of soil and grout generated by the activity. For a
grout trench cutoff wall, this value is the depth of excavation of
the trench made from the surface to contain the additional volume of
soil and grout generated by the activity.
