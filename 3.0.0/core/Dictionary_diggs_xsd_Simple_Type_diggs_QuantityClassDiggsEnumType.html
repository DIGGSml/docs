Annotations
 
Enumeration of DIGGS-specific quantity classes that extend the Energistics Unit of Measure Dictionary. These quantity classes represent physical dimensions or dimensionless ratios specific to geotechnical, geoenvironmental, and related engineering applications that are not included in the standard Energistics dictionary. Each quantity class defines what is being measured (the physical dimension), while units within that class define how it is measured. These extensions are necessary to support specialized measurements in soil mechanics, rock mechanics, groundwater analysis, geophysical testing, and materials characterization common in geotechnical practice.

Facets
enumeration amount of substance per mass 
amount of substance per mass: Molar quantity per unit mass. Used in chemistry, geochemistry, and materials characterization for expressing composition on a molar basis relative to mass. Reciprocal of molecular weight. Important in soil chemistry, contamination studies, and chemical analysis of geomaterials. Base unit equivalent: mol/kg.
enumeration force or mass per volume 
force or mass per volume: Unified quantity class combining force per volume (unit weight) and mass per volume (density). In geotechnical engineering, "density" is often reported as either true mass density or unit weight (force per volume), and this quantity class allows either representation. Important because geotechnical practice uses both conventions depending on regional standards and calculation requirements. Base unit equivalent: N/m³ for unit weight or kg/m³ for mass density.
enumeration gravitational gradient 
gravitational gradient: Rate of change of gravitational acceleration with position. Used extensively in gravity surveys, geophysical investigations, and geodesy. Measured in Eötvös units (E), where 1 E = 10⁻⁹ s⁻² = 0.1 mGal/km. Critical parameter in microgravity surveys for detecting subsurface voids, density variations, and geological structures. Base unit equivalent: 1/s² or E (Eötvös).
enumeration reciprocal time(0.5) 
reciprocal time(0.5): Reciprocal of the square root of time. Used primarily in pressure filtration analysis and consolidation testing where rates vary inversely with square root of time. This quantity class represents filtration coefficients and similar parameters in soil mechanics where drainage follows square-root-of-time behavior. Common in filter cake analysis and consolidation rate calculations. Base unit equivalent: 1/√s or s^-0.5.
enumeration relative rate 
relative rate: Rate of change expressed as percentage per unit time. Used extensively for strain rates in geotechnical testing, creep analysis, and time-dependent deformation. Allows strain rates to be expressed at appropriate time scales from laboratory tests (seconds, minutes) to geological processes (years, millennia). Critical in creep testing, consolidation rate analysis, and long-term deformation monitoring. Base unit equivalent: %/s, %/h, %/year, etc.
enumeration volume or mass loss 
volume or mass loss: Dimensionless ratio representing either volume loss (volume per volume) or mass loss (mass per mass). Used primarily in syneresis testing, shrinkage analysis, and materials characterization where loss can be measured as either volume change or mass change. Allows flexibility in reporting depending on measurement method. Common in clay mineral studies, grout testing, and soil-water interaction analysis. Base unit equivalent: m³/m³ or kg/kg (dimensionless).
